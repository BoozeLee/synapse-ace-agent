# Pilot Brief: Rhythmic Rituals 001

## Working Title

Rhythmic Rituals 001: The Threshold

## One-Line Pitch

A high-taste ritual party where organic house, live percussion, responsive visuals, and simple social ceremonies turn a night out into a shared transformation.

## Pilot City Assumption

Amsterdam or Brussels. Both fit the concept: international creative audiences, strong electronic music culture, intimate venues, and access to wellness-adjacent communities.

## Venue Requirements

- 150 to 250 comfortable capacity
- Strong sound system or permission to bring one
- Low-light control
- Entry area for intention station
- One quiet corner
- Projection wall or LED-friendly surface
- 03:00 or later sound allowance preferred

## Talent

- Resident DJ: taste anchor and continuity
- Guest DJ: main draw
- Percussionist: peak rite and transitions
- Ritual host: short cues only
- Visual operator: phase-responsive visual world
- Photographer/videographer: discreet, consent-aware capture

## Guest Journey

1. Guest enters, receives token, and chooses intention card.
2. Music starts soft and warm.
3. Host gives a short grounding cue.
4. Dance floor gradually opens.
5. Connection station creates social permission.
6. Peak rite aligns percussion, lights, and crowd movement.
7. Release station closes the emotional loop.
8. Afterglow captures playlist, guest fragments, and next invite.

## Budget Skeleton

- Venue: 1,000-3,500
- Sound and lighting: 800-2,500
- Artists and host: 1,500-5,000
- Visuals and design: 500-2,000
- Production crew: 700-2,000
- Content capture: 500-1,500
- Ritual materials: 200-800
- Marketing: 300-1,500

## Ticket Strategy

- Early ritual: 18-24
- Standard: 28-38
- Final: 42-55
- Group circle: 4 to 6 tickets with one reserved arrival slot

## First Sponsors To Approach

- Boutique non-alcoholic drinks
- Tea or cacao brands
- Fragrance or incense brands
- Fashion designers with eveningwear or textile angle
- Audio, projection, or lighting partners
- Boutique hotels and members clubs
