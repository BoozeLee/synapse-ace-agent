# Rhythmic Rituals Operating Plan

## Night Arc

| Phase | Time | Energy | Sound | Ritual Mechanic | Tool Support |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Threshold | 00:00-00:45 | soft arrival | ambient, downtempo, field recordings | guests receive token and choose intention card | Codex SuperLab checklist, Synapse copy generation |
| Grounding | 00:45-01:30 | warm focus | organic house, percussion, low BPM | host gives 90-second room cue | NeuroForge crowd-state model |

## Roles

- Curator: owns concept, booking, audience, and taste.
- Ritual Host: gives short cues and protects the tone from becoming awkward.
- Music Director: aligns DJs, live percussion, and phase transitions.
- Visual Operator: runs projection, generated imagery, and lighting references.
- Floor Producer: watches door, crowd flow, stations, and guest comfort.
- Content Lead: captures media and manages afterglow archive.
- Safety Lead: handles consent, hydration, medical support, and escalation.

## Ritual Stations

### Intention

Guests draw or write one phrase on arrival. The output becomes visual prompt material later in the night.

### Release

A private paper, thread, wax, water, or light-based gesture. It must be optional and fast.

### Connection

Low-pressure prompts that invite pairs or small groups to exchange a sentence, movement, or card.

## Visual System

- Threshold: smoke, mineral textures, slow glyphs, low amber
- Grounding: breathing geometry, skin-tone warmth, candle-like light
- Ignition: botanical movement, textile patterns, gold and green accents
- Peak: high-contrast symbols, pulse-synced strobes, red-white-gold bursts
- Release: deep blue, water, dissolving marks
- Afterglow: pale dawn, soft grain, guest-generated text fragments

## Music Direction

Keep the music credible first. Ritual works only if the room trusts the sound.

- 95-110 BPM arrival
- 110-122 BPM grounding and ignition
- 122-128 BPM peak
- 105-118 BPM release and afterglow

## Safety And Consent

- No forced participation
- Clear sober floor lead
- Visible water and quiet corner
- No photography at ritual stations without explicit consent
- Clear guest code of conduct at ticketing and entry

## Revenue Model

- Tickets: tiered early, standard, final
- Tables or circles: reserved group entry without making the room elitist
- Sponsors: tea, cacao, fragrance, wellness, fashion, audio, visual tech
- Merch: token objects, scarves, incense, limited prints, live-recorded mixes
- Membership: private afterglow archive and priority access
